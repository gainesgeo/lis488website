Volunteer & Leadership Experience

FEAST & ANAD

FEAST (Families Empowered and Supporting the Treatment of Eating Disorders) is an international nonprofit organization which offers support for families of those with eating disorders, connecting loved ones to informational resources and a community of volunteers with shared lived experience. ANAD (National Association of Anorexia Nervosa and Associated Disorders) is a nonprofit organization that focuses on offering free peer-based support to directly to those experiencing eating disorders.

Sibling Support Group Leader, March 2023-present

Through a collaboration between two nonprofit organizations, I provide peer support to people navigating the experience of having a sibling with an eating disorder. This support takes the form of facilitating weekly virtual support groups for people whose siblings currently have eating disorders, offering lived experience after supporting my sister in recovering from anorexia.

Support Group Team Lead, March 2025-present

In my third year of leading support groups, I also volunteered to fill the Team Lead position for the rest of the sibling group leaders, as well as the leaders for two caregiver groups. In this role, I facilitate monthly discussions and supervision meetings to share additional training and resources.

Panelist for webinar "The Effect of Eating Disorders on Siblings: An Authentic Conversation" (2023)

This webinar features myself and two other sibling group leaders in a moderated discussion about how our siblings' diagnoses affected our lives and relationships. It is available to watch in full through YouTube. You can also register for a second FEAST webinar about the sibling experience happening in June 2026.

MindBlossom

MindBlossom is a nonprofit organization that provides mental health education across a variety of topics to support mental health.

Guest Speaker for course “Navigating Eating Disorders: Education & Support Through Expert Guidance” (2024)

I spoke as a panelist in sessions 4 and 12 of this virtual course on my lived experience as a sibling of someone recovered from an eating disorder.
